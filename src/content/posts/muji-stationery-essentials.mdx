MUJI stationery represents the Japanese minimalist approach to desk supplies — items stripped to essential function, neutral aesthetic, durable construction, and accessible pricing. The brand's philosophy of "no brand" (無印) translates to office supplies that work invisibly without dating themselves to trends. We compiled the essential 15-item list from MUJI's stationery catalog, daily-use feedback from minimalist office bloggers, and Japanese productivity culture to give you a complete starter guide.

What distinguishes MUJI from other stationery brands:

**Neutral color palette**: White, off-white, gray, black, beige. No bright primary colors. Items coordinate with any desk aesthetic.

**Minimal branding**: Logos are subtle — usually just the simple "MUJI" text in small print, or barcode-style identification. Items don't shout their origin.

**Functional design**: Each item designed for specific purpose without decorative additions. A pen is a pen. A folder is a folder. Refinement is internal (paper quality, ink flow) rather than external (decorative patterns).

**Universal sizing**: Items follow Japanese stationery standards (A4, A5, B5 for papers; 12 cm rulers; standard polypropylene box dimensions) that combine with other Japanese brands.

**Sustainable materials**: Polypropylene (recyclable), bamboo (renewable), recycled paper. Plastic content minimized where possible.

The cumulative effect: a MUJI-equipped desk looks quiet and intentional rather than busy.

The MUJI gel pen line:

**0.5mm gel ink (recommended starter)**: Smooth writing on most papers. Ink dries in 2-3 seconds. Refillable barrel (replace ink cartridge after 100-150 pages of writing). Color options: black, blue, red, green, light blue, pink. Cost: 3-5 dollars per pen, 25-40 dollars for 10-pack.

**0.7mm gel ink (for bolder lines)**: Same construction with wider tip. Better for forms, signatures, formal documents. Same color options.

**0.38mm gel ink (for precise writing)**: Thinner line. Better for small notebooks (A6 or smaller) or detailed annotation. Some users prefer this for journaling.

**Skip these**: Ballpoint pens (less smooth than gel), expensive specialty inks (gel ink covers most needs), oil-based inks (slower drying than gel).

## Storage System

The MUJI polypropylene storage system is modular:

**File boxes (A4 size)**: Clear polypropylene, 8-12 cm wide. Holds standing files, manuals, project documents. Cost: 8-15 dollars each. Stack on shelves or in cabinets.

**Drawer organizers**: Modular small boxes (10x10, 10x20 cm) that fit together in custom drawer layouts. Used for: pens, paperclips, sticky notes, small tools. Cost: 3-8 dollars each.

**Letter trays (stackable)**: Acrylic or polypropylene. Stack 2-4 high for in-out box, current project, archive. Cost: 10-20 dollars each.

**Pen holders (vertical)**: Wooden or acrylic standing holders. Hold 5-15 pens vertically on desk. Cost: 8-25 dollars.

The system grows organically — start with 2-3 pieces, add as needs require. Don't buy everything at once; you'll discover which items you actually use.

## Digital + Physical Integration

The modern desk balances digital and physical:

**Digital primary, paper backup**: Most active project info digital. Notes that benefit from physical writing (meetings, brainstorming, ideation) go in MUJI notebooks. Reference back via search of notebook (date + topic on top of each page enables search).

**Notebook + scanner workflow**: Write notes physically. Scan important pages (CamScanner, ScanScout apps) for digital archive. Original paper stays for tactile reference; digital copy enables search.

**One active notebook at a time**: Resist the temptation of multiple project notebooks. Use one current notebook for all topics. Move to next notebook when filled. Date pages and use brief topic headers for retrievability.

## Maintenance Mindset

The MUJI-equipped desk requires minimal maintenance:

- **One-in-one-out for pens**: When acquiring new pen, donate or recycle an old one
- **Quarterly drawer audit**: Remove items you haven't touched in 3 months
- **Annual notebook archive**: Move filled notebooks to dedicated storage. Top shelf or drawer for reference.
- **Refill discipline**: Use ink refills rather than buying new pens. Reduces waste and cost.

## What To Avoid

Three patterns counter MUJI minimalism:

**Specialty pen accumulation**: Dozens of pens for different ink colors, line widths, surfaces. Most uses fit 2-3 pens (black 0.5mm, blue 0.5mm, highlighter). Beyond 5-10 pens, accumulating becomes hoarding.

**Buying complete sets without need**: MUJI's storage system is modular. Buy what you need now; expand as use cases emerge. Premade sets often include unused items.

**Mixing aesthetics**: MUJI's neutral palette doesn't combine well with bright office supplies. Choose either MUJI consistency or mix-and-match aesthetic — don't try both.
